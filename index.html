Warriors of Warmth

Heroes for community spaces

When a community space is struggling with poor or unreliable heating, we step in to help by supplying the necessary materials, including a new boiler where needed, and bringing together a team of skilled volunteers to install efficient heating systems and radiators. Our focus is on the local halls, clubs, and community organisations that bring people together and rely on a warm, welcoming environment to stay open and support the people around them.

Find out how we can help

About our work

We're qualified engineers who give our time to help community projects that are struggling with heating: community halls, shelters, places like that. So far we've put in new boilers, heating systems and radiators in halls and meeting spaces across England. The kit comes from our partners; we and other volunteers do the install. People get a warm space they can actually use.

We're a non-profit. No one gets paid. Tradespeople turn up, brands chip in with materials, and we get it done. The community doesn't pay a thing. We just want those places to stay open.

A reliable warm room makes a big difference. We concentrate on the local spaces that need it, and make sure they get the heating that lets them keep the doors open.

Where we work

We operate across England, supporting community spaces in every region.

How it works

Partners supply materials, including boilers. Volunteers provide the labour on the ground.

Who we're here for

Any big or small, local spaces that bring the community together.
